4. Historical Fiction - "All the Light We Cannot See" by Anthony Doerr
Review:
Doerr's Pulitzer Prize-winning novel, "All the Light We Cannot See," is a poignant and beautiful tale of a blind French girl and a German boy whose paths collide in occupied France during World War II. Told with delicate prose and rich historical detail, the book shines a light on the human stories within the enormity of war. This is historical fiction at its most immersive, reminding us of the resilience of the human spirit.

6. Biography - "Steve Jobs" by Walter Isaacson
Review:
Isaacson's biography of Steve Jobs is an unflinching portrait of a tech luminary and a comprehensive look at the life of a man whose vision changed the world. The book delves deep into Jobs's intense personality and inventive spirit, drawing on interviews with friends, family, and Jobs himself. It stands not only as the definitive biography of Jobs but also as a fascinating look at the tech industry and the Silicon Valley ecosystem.
