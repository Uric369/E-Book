8. Self-Help - "Atomic Habits" by James Clear
Review:
In "Atomic Habits," James Clear distills complex behavioral psychology into a guide for making good habits inevitable and bad habits impossible. Clear provides a practical framework for daily self-improvement and a set of simple, actionable strategies for mastering the tiny behaviors that lead to remarkable results. If you're looking to transform your habits, this book is a compelling roadmap to change.

9. Romance - "The Notebook" by Nicholas Sparks
Review:
"The Notebook" is a classic love story that tugs at the heartstrings. Sparks's tale of enduring love is narrated by an elderly man who reads from his faded notebook to his wife with Alzheimer's, recounting the summer they fell in love. The novel's heartfelt and emotional journey through the trials of true love has made it a favorite among romance readers.

10. Horror - "The Haunting of Hill House" by Shirley Jackson
Review:
"The Haunting of Hill House" is a psychological horror masterpiece that has set the standard for ghost stories. Jackson's novel, which follows a group of individuals participating in a paranormal study at a notoriously haunted house, is as much a study of its characters as it is of the supernatural. With its eerie atmosphere and mounting tension, Jackson delivers a chilling exploration of fear and the unknown.
